GDW Exercise 3.1: Gin Rummy

Exercise: Let’s take the classic card game gin rummy. There are two basic procedures to a turn in gin rummy: drawing and discarding.

Take away the discard procedure and try to play the game. What happens?
A: The card-in-hand will increase until all the cards are drawn. And the cards number of each person will be the same. The flexibility of card sets will be reduced.

Now take way both the discard procedure and the draw procedure, and then play the game. What’s missing from the game?
A: The win or lose will be shown at the first round because there will be no change for the cards in hand.

Put the drawing and discarding procedures back, buut take out the rule that says that an opponent can “lay off” unmatched cards to extend the knocker’s sets.
Is the game still playable with this change?
A: No, the “lay off” person can always win at the first round. All the players can’t get playble experience like that.

Now put back the original rules, but take away the objective and play the game again. What happens this time?
A: The player don’t know why they hold the cards and can’t make decision to the rules. Maybe they can produce a new game but not Gin Rummy.

What does the exercise tell us about the formal elements of games?
A game must have included the formal elements. And all the elements Exist side by side and play a part together. Without proper rules, the game can’t be playable. Without a object, players won’t have driving force.
