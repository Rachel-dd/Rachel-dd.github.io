GDW Exercise 3.12: Outcome

Exercise: Name two zero-sum games and two non-zero-sum games. What is the main difference in the outcomes of these games? How does this affect gameplay?

Zero-sum:
HearthStone: Heroes Of Warcraft: Each game round has one winner and one loser. So the main goal of each game is to beat your oppent’s health to 0 by managing your cards.
mahjong: due to different rules, the winner and losers amount can be different. But the sum of the points(or money) in the table are always a constant. So you have to make your
winning combination faster than others in order not to lose points.

Non-zero sum:
Life is stange: It tells a story. No point or money here, you are only faced with some delimmas. But you can feel free to enjoy the story at any time.
Euro Truck Simulator: No win or lose here. You just enjoy driving as a truck driver here.

Zero-sum games also include competion, there’s aways a limitation about time or points or just.. scenarios. That means you can’t easy quit a zero-sum game when you are
competiting with others because this will make you lose or lose something. The Zero-sum game is aways thrilling because while you have the oppotunities to gain some forturn, with
the risk of losing other things.

Non-zero sum game usually don;t have time limit. It’s more flexible to play this kind of game and you have less stress. But the game can also give you stress by putting you in some delimmas.

GDW Exercise 3.10: Conflict

Exercise: Explain how conflict is created in the following games: Tetris, Frogger, Bomberman, Minesweeper, and solitaire.
Does the conflict in these games come from obstacles, opponents, dilemmas, or a combination of these?

Tetris:
You are faced with different shapes of tetris provided by the system, and the speed of its falling can be quick, which you have less time to response.

Obstacles provided by the system
In some latest versions, you can compete with other players. And some of your actions can give blocks to other players. In the same way, you’re faced with more dangers too.
Opponents

Frogger:
You need to type different words to get across the street, and you can’t get rushed by the car.

Obstacles

Bomberman:
Fight with your friends! Your bomb can damage your friend, so do their bomb. The last one survided wins.

Opponents

Minesweeper:
You are faced with unknow bombs in the map. The system gives a random map each round. When you meet the bomb, game over

Obstacles
Sometimes two positions are likely to have one bomb and one safe. There’s no other evidence to show which one is bomb. You need to make choice, live or die.
Dilimmas

Solitaire:
The spread of the cards is different in each round. You might be stuck at any place

Obstacles
Sometimes you need to unfold a card, but if you move some cards away, there might be no return. So you have to make choices
Dilemmas

GDW Exercise 3.8: Utility and Scaricity

Exercise: What are the resources in the games Scrabble and Call of Duty? How are they useful to players? How are they made scarce by the game system?

Scrabble:
Alphabets are recources. Player can gain different scores by combining the alphabets to different words, which makes the alphabets useful.
And each alphabet is restrited to only use once(if they are presents on the table), so it has scarcity because you should use limited number(and position) of alphabets to win scores.

Call of Duty:
resources: weapons
Weapons have different damages, range and speed, which have different Injury efficiency to different kind of enemies. > utility
Weapons and ammos are limited, guns are not everywhere, you should find them and equipped with ammos. > Scarcity
