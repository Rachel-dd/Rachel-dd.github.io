GDW Exercise 3.5: Procedures for Blackjack

Exercise: List the procedures for blackjack. Be specific. What is the starting action? The progression of action? Any special actions? The resolving action?

Start action: (Among players, we should choose the Banker. Usually by turns or dice.) Banker holds all the cards first. The banker deal 2 cards clockwise to each player at the beginning. Game starts
GET CARD or stop card: start with the person on Banker’s left one by one. The player can choose to get 1 card at each round from the banker, or just stop getting card in the play. The one who choose to stop can’t get card anymore
Special action-Boom: If sum>21, the player lose. He has to show off all the cards
Special action-blackjack: Wins
resolving action: when all the non-boom players stop the cards, all the players(include the banker) show off the cards and compare the points. Blankjack>21>other. The biggest wins.

GDW Exercise 3.1: Gin Rummy

Exercise: Let’s take the classic card game gin rummy. There are two basic procedures to a turn in gin rummy: drawing and discarding.

Take away the discard procedure and try to play the game. What happens?
A: The card-in-hand will increase until all the cards are drawn. And the cards number of each person will be the same. The flexibility of card sets will be reduced.

Now take way both the discard procedure and the draw procedure, and then play the game. What’s missing from the game?
A: The win or lose will be shown at the first round because there will be no change for the cards in hand.

Put the drawing and discarding procedures back, buut take out the rule that says that an opponent can “lay off” unmatched cards to extend the knocker’s sets.
Is the game still playable with this change?
A: No, the “lay off” person can always win at the first round. All the players can’t get playble experience like that.

Now put back the original rules, but take away the objective and play the game again. What happens this time?
A: The player don’t know why they hold the cards and can’t make decision to the rules. Maybe they can produce a new game but not Gin Rummy.

What does the exercise tell us about the formal elements of games?
A game must have included the formal elements. And all the elements Exist side by side and play a part together. Without proper rules, the game can’t be playable. Without a object, players won’t have driving force.

GDW Exercise 2.7: Premise

Exercise: What are the premises for the game Risk, Clue, Pit, and Guitar Hero? If you don’t know these games, pick games that you are more familiar with.

  1. Age of Empires
    You are a Feudal lord, start with some land and some people. Some other tribes are around you, they might be offensive or friendly.
    You can trade, harvest, manage army and fight or do everything to make your country bigger and better

  2. 大富翁
    You are in a competition with some other protential rich people. You can buy and sell houses and items to gain money, and also Add barriers to others so that others lose money.
    Your goal is to be the richest and let others be as poor as you can.

  3. 逆転裁判
    You’re a laywer that might meet different types of cases. You can investigate the clues carefully to reveal the truth, and you can inhance the friendship with other characters.
    Your goal is to Find out the real murderer in each case. Don’t let the innocent die.

  4. Just Dance
    Enjoy the dance alone or with your friends. Put your hands up and move your legs! Try to gain more scores by doing the perfect actions as sample.
