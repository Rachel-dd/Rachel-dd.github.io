GDW Exercise 3.10: Conflict

Exercise: Explain how conflict is created in the following games: Tetris, Frogger, Bomberman, Minesweeper, and solitaire.
Does the conflict in these games come from obstacles, opponents, dilemmas, or a combination of these?

Tetris:
You are faced with different shapes of tetris provided by the system, and the speed of its falling can be quick, which you have less time to response.

Obstacles provided by the system
In some latest versions, you can compete with other players. And some of your actions can give blocks to other players. In the same way, you’re faced with more dangers too.
Opponents

Frogger:
You need to type different words to get across the street, and you can’t get rushed by the car.

Obstacles

Bomberman:
Fight with your friends! Your bomb can damage your friend, so do their bomb. The last one survided wins.

Opponents

Minesweeper:
You are faced with unknow bombs in the map. The system gives a random map each round. When you meet the bomb, game over

Obstacles
Sometimes two positions are likely to have one bomb and one safe. There’s no other evidence to show which one is bomb. You need to make choice, live or die.
Dilimmas

Solitaire:
The spread of the cards is different in each round. You might be stuck at any place

Obstacles
Sometimes you need to unfold a card, but if you move some cards away, there might be no return. So you have to make choices

GDW Exercise 3.5: Procedures for Blackjack

Exercise: List the procedures for blackjack. Be specific. What is the starting action? The progression of action? Any special actions? The resolving action?

Start action: (Among players, we should choose the Banker. Usually by turns or dice.) Banker holds all the cards first. The banker deal 2 cards clockwise to each player at the beginning. Game starts
GET CARD or stop card: start with the person on Banker’s left one by one. The player can choose to get 1 card at each round from the banker, or just stop getting card in the play. The one who choose to stop can’t get card anymore
Special action-Boom: If sum>21, the player lose. He has to show off all the cards
Special action-blackjack: Wins
resolving action: when all the non-boom players stop the cards, all the players(include the banker) show off the cards and compare the points. Blankjack>21>other. The biggest wins.
